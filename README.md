# Education for All: Donor Insights & Strategic Fundraising Report

## 📌 One-Line Description
A data-driven evaluation of the "Education for All" donor database using PostgreSQL and interactive dashboards to optimize fundraising campaigns and maximize donor lifetime value.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem Statement
The "Education for All" initiative provides underprivileged children with access to quality education. While fundraising operations have successfully generated database records, the organization lacks a structured, analytical profile of its donor base. 

To maximize fundraising efficiency, prevent donor fatigue, and optimize acquisition, this project addresses the following objectives:
1. **Donor Behavior Profiling:** Uncovering giving frequency trends and identifying high-value demographic cohorts.
2. **Donor Retention:** Identifying key churn indicators to increase overall donor lifetime value.
3. **Acquisition & Stewardship:** Creating a targeted marketing roadmap by mapping donor demographics (e.g., job fields, geographic hubs, lifestyle indicators) to contributions.

---

## 📊 Dataset Description
The database is structured relational schema utilizing mock tracking data over a multi-year timeline containing **1,000 unique donor interactions**:
* **`Donation_Data` (Table):** Captures individual transaction details, including transaction ID, first/last names, email addresses, gender, professional job field, donation amount, state of residence, and shirt size.
* **`Donor_Data` (Table):** Captures ongoing donor relationship and profile metrics, including unique donor ID, recurring donation frequency (e.g., Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Yearly, Once), university affiliation, vehicle make, second language, favorite color, and preferred movie genre.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
* **Database Management System (DBMS):** PostgreSQL (v15+) for data structuring, filtering, cohort division, schema joins, and view configurations.
* **Visualization Tools:** Microsoft Excel / Power BI (Interactive Donor Performance Dashboards).
* **Documentation & Modeling:** QuickDBD (Entity-Relationship Diagram), Microsoft Word / PowerPoint (Executive Reporting).

---

## 📈 Key Findings
* **High-Value Concentration:** A critical **65% of total capital ($162,350.00)** is generated by a high-value tier of donors giving more than $200 per transaction, representing a minority of total interactions (410 donations).
* **Corporate Demographics:** Professional corporate employees in **Business Development** and **Engineering** drive the highest organizational yields, contributing $42,150.00 and $38,900.00 respectively.
* **Geographical Hotspots:** Giving performance is heavily concentrated in major metropolitan hubs, led by **California ($34,500.00)** and **New York ($29,100.00)**.
* **Lifestyle Correlation:** High-value donors exhibit strong affinity indicators, displaying a profound statistical correlation with owners of premium car brands (such as **Tesla, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz**).

---

## 🎯 Strategic Recommendations
* **Establish a Premium Tier ('Chancellor's Circle'):** Direct high-touch stewardship efforts specifically toward donors in high-yielding corporate sectors (Business Development, Engineering, Legal) who match the high-value profile.
* **Optimize Geographic Spend:** Target physical donor drives and marketing budgets to high-performing states (**CA, NY, TX**), while shifting low-yield states (e.g., Wyoming, Vermont) to low-cost automated email engagement.
* **Lifestyle Affinity Partnerships:** Build targeted charity events and marketing promotions aligning with premium brands (e.g., green-energy/EV test drive charity events) to capture high-affinity prospects.
